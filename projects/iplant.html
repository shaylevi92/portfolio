Overview

iPlant is a smart dashboard designed to help greenhouse growers monitor the condition of their plants. It collects real-time data on soil quality, sunlight, temperature, and nutrients. This allows the grower to detect anomalies early and provide the plants with exactly what they need to thrive.

Overview Image Placeholder

Research

My research process included interviewing farmers and greenhouse workers, visiting nurseries, and observing how growers currently track plant health. I identified major challenges such as the lack of centralized data, time-consuming manual checks, and delayed responses to issues like pests or irrigation problems.

Wireframes

Before designing the final UI, I created a set of low-fidelity wireframes to explore the main information flow and structure. I focused on simplifying the dashboard layout while ensuring access to all critical plant data.

Wireframes Image Placeholder

Final Design

The final interface presents plant health data in a clean, color-coded layout. It includes alert cards, condition summaries, and historical charts. I used calm greens and neutral tones to create a sense of clarity and focus for the user.

iPlant taught me how to combine real-world fieldwork with digital product thinking. It strengthened my ability to translate user needs into structured, intuitive interfaces and inspired me to continue building tools that make complex systems more accessible.
